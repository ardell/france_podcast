# Episode ideas — not yet written

Backlog of episodes we've discussed but haven't produced. Each lists the
physical doorway, the through-line, and the recurring theme(s) it carries.
Format, tone, and workflow are defined in `CLAUDE.md`. To produce one, just
ask; it gets written to `episodes/text/NN-slug.txt`, built, and published.

Numbering note: `NN-` prefixes set listening order. Written so far: 01 (TGV),
02 (Café), 03 (Guillotine), 04 (Revolution Changed), 05 (Laïcité), 06 (Arles as
Rome), 07 (Van Gogh), 08 (Les Alyscamps), 09 (City of Popes), 10 (Pont du Gard).
The slugs below reserve the next numbers in trip order.

## River stops (Viviers / Tournon / Vienne)

- **11 — The Rhône as a Highway** *(river days)*
  Doorway: the river itself, swift and historically brutal to travel upstream
  (boats hauled up by men and horses). Through-line: the Rhône as the great
  north–south trade and cultural corridor — how Greek then Roman civilization
  came up from Massalia (Marseille), why every stop on this cruise is a river
  town. Modern chapter: the Compagnie Nationale du Rhône dams/locks (1930s on)
  that let the ship glide upstream today. Themes: distributed-vs-centralized
  power (the river's organic order vs. Paris's hub-and-spoke), everyday economy,
  France-vs-America (the CNR echoes the TVA and the Columbia/Colorado dams,
  all 1933). *Was researched but never drafted — start here.*

- **11-alt — Côtes du Rhône: A River You Can Drink** *(Tournon)*
  Doorway: the steep vineyards on the banks. Northern Rhône (Syrah; Hermitage,
  Côte-Rôtie — at Tournon) vs. southern Rhône (Grenache-led blends;
  Châteauneuf-du-Pape). What to try, food pairings. Theme: food & wine as
  culture. *(Overlaps the dedicated wine episode below — decide whether to
  merge or keep the wine episode broader/national.)*

- **12 — The Truffle and the Terroir** *(Viviers / Grignan)*
  Doorway: the "black diamond" winter truffle of the Tricastin/Grignan area.
  What terroir actually claims — the idea that a place is tasteable, and how
  France built a legal and cultural apparatus (AOC) around it. Themes: food,
  land & identity, the endurance of everyday life.

- **13 — Vienne, Rome, and Jazz** *(Vienne)*
  Doorway: the Temple of Augustus and Livia, one of the best-preserved Roman
  temples anywhere, standing in the middle of town. Plus Vienne's celebrated
  summer jazz festival in the Roman theater (ties to the listener's love of
  jazz). Themes: Rome, the endurance of everyday culture, high culture in a
  provincial town.

## Lyon

- **14 — The Capital of Eating** *(Lyon)*
  Doorway: a bouchon, the traditional Lyonnais eatery. The mères lyonnaises
  (the women cooks who built Lyon's reputation), Paul Bocuse, Les Halles.
  Why Lyon and not Paris is called France's food capital. Themes: food as
  culture, everyday endurance.

- **15 — Silk, Revolt, and the Canuts** *(Lyon)*
  Doorway: the Croix-Rousse hill and the traboules (covered passages). The
  canuts (silk weavers) and their uprisings of 1831 and 1834 — among the first
  modern industrial-worker revolts. American echoes (early labor history).
  Themes: the fate of classes, distributed power, everyday life.

- **16 — Fourvière and the Two Hills** *(Lyon)*
  Doorway: the Basilica of Notre-Dame de Fourvière on "the hill that prays,"
  looking across to Croix-Rousse, "the hill that works." Beneath it, Roman
  Lugdunum — capital of the Three Gauls, birthplace of emperors. Themes:
  church vs. state, Rome, centralized power.

## Thematic (not tied to one stop)

- **17 — Provençal vs. Lyonnais vs. Parisian Cooking** *(food)*
  Olive oil and the sun (Provence) vs. butter, cream, pork, and the river
  (Lyon) vs. the Parisian bistro and haute-cuisine tradition. Why the same
  country cooks in three such different registers, and what that says about
  regional France. Theme: food, regional identity, north–south France.

- **18 — French Wine (personalized)** *(wine)*
  Tailored to the listener's taste: smooth, earthy, big reds and
  smooth/balanced/buttery whites. What's special in the regions actually
  traveled (Rhône, Provence, Beaujolais near Lyon), specific bottles to try,
  and food pairings. Theme: food & wine as a doorway into culture.
  *(Decide relationship to 11-alt Côtes du Rhône — likely keep this one broader
  and let the Côtes du Rhône episode go deep on the river's own wines.)*
